  1. Efficient Defense Against First Order Adversarial Attacks on Convolutional Neural Networks

    Authors: Sadia Afrose, Subah Karnine, Dr. Hafiz Imtiaz

    Supervisor: Dr. Hafiz Imtiaz, Professor, BUET

    Submitted In: Signal, Image and Video Processing Journal by Springer. (Under Review)

    Proposed a computational-efficient approach to enhance the robustness of machine learning models against adversarial attacks, where inputs are purposefully altered to induce incorrect predictions. Focused on identifying the parts of the model most vulnerable to adversarial attacks and selectively trained only these sensitive components. This approach achieved equivalent results comparable to conventional full-model adversarial training while using fewer resources, ensuring significant computational load savings.

  2. Aquatic Robot for Trash Removal and Water Quality Monitoring Built from Recycled Materials

    Authors: S. Afrose, S.K. Das, S. Saha, F. Fardaous, R. Mashtura

    Presented At: 13th International Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (ICECE 2024). (Accepted)

    Developed an aquatic robot designed to remove trash and monitor water quality. The robot was built using recycled materials, promoting sustainability and efficient use of resources. The system successfully demonstrated its functionality in addressing environmental challenges related to water pollution.
